Methodology

How ReviewGuard
detects fake reviews

No black boxes. Here's exactly how our rule-based detection engine works, signal by signal.

01
Text Ingestion & Preprocessing
The review text is cleaned and tokenized. Words are normalized to lowercase, punctuation patterns are preserved (they carry signal), and character counts are recorded.
NLP
02
Language Pattern Analysis
We scan for superlative overload (4+ extreme adjectives), repetitive word usage (same key words ≥3 times), vague generic phrases like "very good" and "recommend everyone", and excessive capitalization.
Language
03
Sentiment Authenticity Check
Genuine reviews almost always contain at least one qualification or minor criticism. We check for the absence of any negative sentiment in high-rating reviews — a strong fake signal.
Sentiment
04
Specificity & Detail Scan
Real users mention price paid (₹1499), usage duration ("3 weeks"), product variants ("64GB model"), or comparisons ("unlike my old phone"). We check if any such specifics exist in the text.
Detail
05
Behavioral Pattern Matching
Fake reviews often praise the seller's delivery speed more than the product itself. We detect excessive seller mentions, generic reviewer usernames, and short unsubstantiated submissions.
Behavior
06
Weighted Score Aggregation
Each triggered signal adds (or subtracts, for genuine indicators) a weighted value to the total fake score. The final score is normalized to 0–100, and a verdict is assigned at thresholds 38 and 65.

Score Interpretation

What the fake score means

0–37
Likely Genuine ✅
Few or no red flags detected. The review shows language patterns and detail levels consistent with real user experience. Proceed with moderate confidence.
38–64
Suspicious ⚠️
Some concerning patterns present. Not definitively fake, but cross-reference with other reviews. Look for a spread of ratings from different reviewer profiles.
65–100
Likely Fake 🚨
Multiple strong fake signals triggered. This review has a high probability of being incentivized, bot-generated, or written by a paid reviewer network.
⚠️
Important Limitations
ReviewGuard is a heuristic tool, not a definitive verdict machine. Some genuine reviews may score poorly (e.g. a non-native English speaker writing simply) and some sophisticated fakes may score well. Always treat the result as one data point among many. The most reliable approach is to read a sample of reviews across multiple star ratings before making any purchase decision.
